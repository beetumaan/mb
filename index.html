How It Began

our story

A common friend. A casual hello. And somehow, within days, our thoughts started finishing each other's sentences and our vibes started speaking the same language.

Then came Rishikesh.

The Ganga must have heard something we couldn't yet say out loud — because that trip didn't just deepen us, it decided us. Distance came after, kilometres of it, but we turned every gap into a reason for another trip, another memory, another small adventure that pulled us closer instead of pushing us apart.

Four years later, we're still that couple — finding each other on every map.

From Beetu, To Muskan

a letter

us & some lights ♡
My Muskan,

I'm not really a poetry person. You know that better than anyone. So I'll just write what's in my heart, the way I'd say it if you were sitting next to me right now.

I see you worrying. About December. About the rituals, about the families, about how two castes can sit at the same mandap without the world pulling at the seams. I see it in your eyes even when you don't say it.

So please — breathe.

We are not December 12th. We are not three days of ceremonies. We are not the customs or the relatives or the whispers in the room. We are four years of small moments — the way our thoughts matched before we even knew we were matching, the trips we planned just to close the distance, the way Rishikesh held us up when miles tried to tear us down. That is us. The wedding is just the world finally catching up.

Here's my one promise for those three wedding days: you and I — we don't carry their weight. We let them happen. We laugh, we eat, we cry a little, and we let the families do their thing. Because the day after, and the day after that, and the next forty thousand days — those belong to us. And I need you fresh for those. I need you well. I need my Muskan, not a Muskan who wore herself out trying to make everyone else's three days perfect.

Inter-caste, inter-anything, inter-whatever — they can write their rules. We've already broken the only one that mattered: we found each other anyway.

I love you on April 26th. I'll love you on December 12th. I'll love you on every ordinary Tuesday in between, and on every Tuesday for the rest of our lives.

Be calm, jaan. I've got us.

Always & forever yours,
Beetu
